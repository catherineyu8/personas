I went to local cafes and observed baristas as they navigated morning rush hours and slower moments throughout their shifts. I also interviewed home baristas, who used their own espresso machines to make a cafe-worthy drink-of-the-day.

Key Observations:

  • Move relatively fluidly throughout the entire process
  • Some users tamped the coffee grounds and steamed milk effortlessly (both manual steps)
  • Others took a longer time & more care to tamp, sometimes several times
  • Use force to attach the portafilter to the machine and ensure it is locked in place
  • After attaching the portafilter, quickly press the shot button to start the shot extraction
  • Holds a hand under portafilter after shot extraction when removing the grounds, as the portafilter continues dripping occasionally
  • Constantly wiping down parts of the machine such as the steam wand, drip tray

Interview Responses:

1. What were your expectations on the difficulty level of using this machine, when you first started using it?
  • For a simpler espresso machine - thought it would be easy to use
  • For a more complex espresso machine - initially intimidated - thought it would be challenging to learn the many controls/features
  • Steep learning curve, but gets easier after practice
  • Experienced users - expected to very easily know how to use the interface
2. What interactive parts/features do you expect to find in an espresso machine like this?
  • Buttons/touchscreen to make different types of drinks
  • Display of the shot time/temp/pressure
  • Buttons/dials to adjust grind size, shot time/temp
  • Indicators for water level + cleaning status
  • Portafilter, to hold espresso
  • Steaming wand
3. How often do you use this machine, and what do you use it for?
  • Barista - used every couple minutes throughout a work shift
  • Home espresso machine owner - used daily (once, sometimes twice)
  • To make espresso-based drinks
  • To steam milk for other hot beverages
  • To get hot water for non milk-based drinks
4. Can you describe the steps you take when using this machine?
  • Check water level and amount of beans in grinder (refill either as needed)
  • Grind beans by pressing a button
  • Tamp grounds (manual step)
  • Attach portafilter to main machine body and lock in place
  • Press a button or pull lever to extract espresso shot
  • Steam milk (manual step) by turning steam wand on/off using a switch, clean wand by wiping down and purging leftover steam
  • Empty portafilter - unlock it, dump out puck, rinse out using hot water function and wipe dry
  • Occasionally clean the machine - dump out/wash water reservoir
  • For simpler machines/depending on the type of drink made, some of these steps were skipped/simplified
5. Is it easy or difficult to use this machine? What made it that way?
  • Manual steps are hard to get correct, but gets easier to do with practice
  • Very easy/intuitive to use the basic features of the interface
    • Just press buttons, machine automates the espresso-making process
    • Display helps provide feedback/monitor how well the drink is made (shot time/temp/pressure)
  • Additional features make it more complicated to understand + use
  • Some users - overwhelming to use the machine at first
    • Unclear button labels
    • Needed to read user manual to understand all the settings
  • Hard to understand how the components of the machine work together to make a good drink
6. Have you ever made a mistake when using the machine? If so, what happened, and how did you fix it?
  • Generally, any mistakes made would result in adjusting some of the machine settings and having to restart the entire espresso-making process
  • Mistakes included:
    • Grinding an incorrect amount of coffee
    • Portafilter not locked in, it popped out and coffee sprayed everywhere
    • Overfilled water tank - machine wouldn't start
    • Not enough water in the tank, machine made horrible noise and didn't extract a shot
    • Mistakes were common during the manual process, such as extracting a bad shot or incorrectly steaming milk
7. Was there ever a time when the machine didn't work as expected? What did you do?
  • Some users - no problems at all
  • Water doesn't come out of machine at all
    • Test all the different water features, look up the issue online
    • Fix - take out water filter + dump out water, attach filter first before refilling tank
    • Another fix - properly lock water reservoir into place
  • Improper shot
    • Due to incorrect manual steps with grind size/tamping
      • If shot under extracted (according to display) → adjust dial to a finer grind
      • If shot over extracted (according to display) → adjust dial to a coarser grind
    • Espresso flowed too fast - would ideally like to increase extraction time, but instead (due to machine setting limitations) sets grind finer
