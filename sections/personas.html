Based on the observations and interview responses, I determined two main types of user experience with espresso machines. Some users effortlessly perform all the steps of extracting an espresso shot, while others move a little more unsurely. To better capture these perspectives, I crafted two user personas to represent these types of users.

Persona 1: Home Barista

Thinks Feels
  • I'm so tired
  • Did I tamp it evenly enough?
  • I hope I don't mess up the tamping again
  • I thought I tamped it harder than last time, but why is it still under extracted?
  • In a rush
  • Frustrated by the complex components that contribute to a good espresso
  • Unsure about whether they tamped the coffee hard and even enough
  • Confused about what is causing the espresso shot to be pulled incorrectly
  • Excited by their morning coffee
Says Does
  • Oh no, I'm late! (again)
  • Aw man, I didn't tamp the coffee grounds hard enough again
  • Why did just a little bit of espresso come out?
  • I can't believe my espresso machine decided to stop working when I'm late!
  • Watches tutorials on how to properly use the machine
  • Compares their daily drink to one from a cafe
  • Sticks to the same grind settings as long as the espresso is “good enough” but not necessarily perfect

The home barista is a college student using the espresso machine to quickly and easily get their daily caffeine fix, while still making a delicious drink. They don't understand all of the specific details about the espresso-making process, but want to have the same experience and drink quality as a cafe barista by using the portafilter to pull a high-quality espresso.

This persona reflects the less-experienced users of the interface, who are looking for a balance between ease of use and quality of the espresso made using the machine. They rely on the preset settings of the machine to easily automatically extract espresso that still tastes like it was made expertly in a cafe.

Persona 2: Cafe Barista

Thinks Feels
  • This machine is limiting my coffee-making abilities!
  • Pre-programmed settings don't always work - every type of coffee bean is different!
  • We're especially busy this morning - I'm glad I can make espresso so fast!
  • Confident in knowing when and why an espresso shot doesn't turn out quite right
  • Unsure that a basic espresso machine can produce consistent quality espresso
  • Stressed during the morning rush when they have to keep on making drinks nonstop
Says Does
  • I need to shorten the extraction time
  • “Iced oat latte for Emma~”
  • This steam wand isn't very powerful - it takes so long to steam milk
  • I'm never using a home-style espresso machine again - I can't control anything!
  • Tries hacks they see online to manually adjust the coffee machine's preset settings
  • Weighs the amount espresso grounds used, the amount of espresso extracted, and times how long the extraction takes
  • Compares their espresso to espresso tastings they've done and know are good

The cafe barista is a middle-aged person who primarily works at a coffee shop. They understand many of the underlying complexities that go into making a good espresso shot, and aim to consistently and efficiently pull high-quality espresso shots. Their performance is limited by the lack of customization over controls such as extraction pressure and time on a standard espresso machine used at home.

This persona reflects the frequent, experienced users of the espresso machine. They also want quickly to produce quality espresso, but understand the complexities that make up a good shot and thus prefer customizable settings over a simpler interface.
